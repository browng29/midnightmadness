The Midnight Madness Project

What is Midnight Madness?

Midnight madness is a film screening programme part of the Toronto International Film Festival, or TIFF, consisting of a selection of action, horror, science fiction, and fantasy cinema. In other words, genre film.

At my first TIFF, I fell in love with Midnight Madness. The movies reminded me of those I would watch with my friends at sleepovers, and the midnight screening time certainly added to that. The films were some of the most fun, most adventurous of the festival and I was instantly captivated.

After returning home from Toronto, I was eager to dive into the history of the festival, in hopes of finding similar films to enjoy. To my surprise, there was a lack of organized information on not only the history of Midnight Madness, but also the history of TIFF itself. I sought out to fill this gap in the recording of Midnight Madness history, as well as to create an online and readily available resource for those who, like me, are interested in finding out more information about one of TIFF's most colorful programmes.

Enter: The Project

My goals for this project are threefold:

  1. To create a searchable database of all the films shown in the history of Midnight Madness at TIFF.
  2. To provide a space for fans of the programme to learn more about the films they love.
  3. To create an online resource for Midnight Madness related scholarship.
